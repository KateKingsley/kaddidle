Nitrogen Cycle
Atmospheric N2
Lightning fixes atmospheric nitrogen with water forming nitric acid HNO3 that falls to the ground and is absorbed by the soil.


Nitrogen from Animals
Urea CO(NH2)2 is found in body fluids such as sweat and waste.


Nitrogen from Decay
Decay and break down of animal waste, and formally living organisms will produce ammonia NH4. When mixed with water ammonia becomes ammonium ions NH4+


Nitrogen from Bacteria fixation
Found on the roots of alfalfa, beans, legumes, and peas, the bacteria remove hydrogen from ammonia releasing the nitrogen in a useable form for the plants.

Other soil bacteria convert nitrite ions NO2 into nitrate ions NO3-
